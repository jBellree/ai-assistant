# Tutoring Notes: Linking the SA100 and Company Accounts

---

## Chunk 8: Linking the SA100 and company accounts together

Neither document tells the full story on its own. Together they do. This is the most important skill the team needs to develop.

### The problem

A Ltd co director wants to borrow £150k. SA100 shows total income £45k. Lender sees 3x income borrowing and thinks no. But £45k is just what was declared personally. The real picture can be completely different.

### Worked example — James, sole director of a consultancy Ltd co, financing a £180k McLaren

**SA100 shows:**
- PAYE salary: £12,500
- Dividends: £37,500
- Total declared income: £50,000

Looks thin. Now open the company accounts.

**Company accounts show:**
- Turnover: £380,000
- Profit after tax: £95,000
- Retained earnings: £210,000
- DLA: credit balance £45,000 (company owes James — he's been lending the company money)
- Director salary in overheads: £12,500 (matches SA100)
- Dividends paid: £37,500 (matches SA100)

**The real picture:**
- Company turned over £380k and made £95k profit after tax
- James took a conservative £50k personally — well below what the company could support
- £210k retained earnings sitting in the company — he could draw significantly more if needed
- Credit DLA shows James has been putting money in, not stripping it out
- £50k personal income is a choice, not a ceiling

**The narrative for the underwriter:**
"Director of a profitable consultancy turning over £380k. Deliberately conservative on personal drawings. £210k retained earnings available. Strong company balance sheet. Personal income of £50k is a choice, not a ceiling."

That's painting the picture. Not just reading the number.

### The checklist — when you have both documents

Work through in order:

1. **Match the salary** — PAYE salary on SA100 should match director salary in company overheads.
2. **Match the dividends** — dividends on SA100 should match dividends paid in company accounts.
3. **Check the gap** — what could the company have paid vs what did the director actually take? Big gap = conservative drawings = positive story.
4. **Check retained earnings** — meaningful pot? Supports the director's ability to draw more if needed.
5. **Check the DLA** — credit or overdrawn? Which direction, what size?
6. **Build the one-liner** — what is the headline strength? Write that first in the proposal.

### When the numbers don't match

If SA100 dividends don't match the company accounts, or salary figures differ — stop. Query with the customer or accountant before submitting. Mismatching figures raise immediate red flags with underwriters and can kill a deal.
