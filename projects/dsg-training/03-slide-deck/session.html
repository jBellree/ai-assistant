Scenario A — Verdict
Scenario A — Do Not Submit

What looked strong on the surface

The trap

  • Shareholder funds of £793k are buildings and machinery. They cannot make a monthly car payment.
  • The asset-rich, cash-poor company. Credit Safe loves it. The underwriter won't once they see the P&L.
  • Profit after tax of £5,600 cannot support dividends. Personal income is £12,500 only.
  • Salary mismatch between SA100 and accounts — a question that must be answered before you submit anything.
  • Overdrawn DLA — director already owes his company money.

The lesson

  • Micro accounts show shareholder funds — but not where those funds are tied up.
  • Always look at the P&L. A company can look wealthy and be cash-poor.
  • Cross-reference the SA100 salary against the company accounts. They must match.
  • An overdrawn DLA is a liability. Hidden inside "creditors" on micro accounts — you have to ask for the full accounts to find it.

Judgement Framework

Do I need the full accounts?

You won't always have a full set. Ask three questions — if two are clearly strong, you can often proceed with what you have.

1. Is the lender asset-secure?

  • 10% deposit — not enough on any vehicle
  • 50%+ deposit — lender is covered
  • 20-30% — depends on vehicle, term, depreciation
  • Short term (2 years) helps significantly
  • Prestige exotics hold value better than mainstream

2. Does credit support the story?

  • Clean history, long credit file — less pressure on accounts to do the heavy lifting
  • Blips on credit — accounts need to compensate. Get the full picture.
  • CCJs or defaults — always get full accounts before submitting

3. What do micro accounts suggest?

  • Strong shareholder funds, long trading history — full accounts likely support this
  • Check what's inside the creditor line if you can
  • Young company or unclear balance sheet — get the full accounts first

If two of the three are clearly strong — reasonable to proceed. If two are uncertain or weak — ask for the full accounts before submitting.

Quick Reference

Red flags and green flags — at a glance

Red Flags

  • SA100 salary doesn't match director remuneration in company accounts
  • Strong shareholder funds but minimal profit (asset-rich, cash-poor)
  • Overdrawn DLA — director owes the company
  • Dividends exceed profit after tax with no retained earnings to support them
  • Tax Year Overview shows unpaid tax bill
  • Company incorporated under 2 years
  • Negative equity (liabilities exceed assets)

Green Flags

  • SA100 and company accounts cross-reference cleanly
  • Dividends well below profit after tax — room to draw more
  • Credit DLA — director has personally supported the company
  • Strong retained earnings — profitable history, not stripping cash
  • Tax paid and up to date
  • Long trading history (5+ years)
  • Low personal drawings relative to company profit — conservative director

The Proposal Structure

  • Headline — one line, the strongest thing about this deal
  • Customer — 3-5 bullets, facts only
  • The deal — asset, price, deposit, amount, term, balloon
  • Weakness — brief, honest, with context
  • The ask — one line

The Cross-Reference Checklist

  • Match salary: SA100 vs company accounts
  • Match dividends: SA100 vs company accounts
  • Check the gap: what could the company have paid vs what was taken?
  • Check retained earnings: is there a pot?
  • Check DLA: credit or overdrawn, what size?
  • Build the headline: if you can't write it, go back to the documents
