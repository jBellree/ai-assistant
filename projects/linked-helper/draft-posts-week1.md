## POST 1 — Monday (Educational)

HP vs PCP vs HP with a Balloon.

Three letters. Three very different agreements. And most people are never given a clear explanation of how they actually work.

Here is the simplest breakdown I can give you after nearly 30 years in vehicle finance:

HP (Hire Purchase) — Fixed monthly payments over a set term. At the end, the vehicle is yours. No mileage restrictions. Straightforward ownership.

PCP (Personal Contract Purchase) — Lower monthly payments because part of the vehicle's value is deferred to the end. At the end, you can keep it, return it, or part exchange. Flexible, but you need to understand the mileage limits and final payment.

HP with a Balloon (Lease Purchase) — A more specialist agreement, typically for higher value vehicles. Monthly payments are structured around a final balloon, giving you flexibility in how you manage the end of the deal.

Each one has its place. The right choice depends on your circumstances, your plans, and how long you intend to keep the vehicle.

A finance deal should fit your plans, not the other way around.

## POST 3 — Thursday (Myth-Busting)

A good finance deal is NOT just about the lowest monthly payment.

I hear this all the time. People compare deals purely on what they pay each month. And I understand why — it is the number that hits your bank account every 30 days.

But here is what that figure does not tell you:

What is the total amount payable over the full term?
What happens at the end of the agreement?
Is there a balloon payment you need to plan for?
What are the mileage restrictions and what happens if you exceed them?
Can you exit early, and if so, at what cost?

Two deals can look identical on a monthly basis and be completely different when you look at the full picture.

The numbers never lie. But they do need explaining.

Before you commit to any vehicle finance agreement, make sure you understand not just how you enter the deal, but how you exit it.

That is where clarity makes all the difference.
