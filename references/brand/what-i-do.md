**The products in plain English**

- **HP** – Hire Purchase is a simple way to finance your car. You pay for your car over fixed monthly payments (2-5 years), and ownership transfers to you at the end. Payments and interest rates are fixed from the start, with no surprises. You can also opt for a ‘final balloon payment’ to reduce monthly costs.
	- **What are the benefits**
		- HP offers flexible deposit options and fixed monthly payments, making it a straightforward way to own your car. Unlike PCP, there are no annual mileage restrictions. Customise your agreement length to suit your finances and get the keys to your dream car without hassle.
	- **What are the risks**
		- Remember, you’re legally bound to the terms of the HP agreement. Missing payments can affect your credit score and lead to repossession if you’ve paid less than a third of the total amount. Always consider your ability to keep up with repayments.
- **PCP** – PCP finance (**Personal Contract Purchase**) is a cost-effective way to finance a car with a flexible deposit and three end of contract options: keep, trade, or return. Originally introduced in the late 90s, it defers the car’s value (**Guaranteed Minimum Future Value/GMFV**) until the end of the contract, reducing monthly payments over traditional Hire Purchase. This kind of agreement is typically taken over 4 years (48 months) with an average deposit of 10%, although a deposit isn’t always required, the lenders available to you may reduce if you choose not to add a deposit.
	- **What are the benefits**
		- PCP offers consistent, lower monthly payments due to the GMFV reducing the amount you actually pay off, along with a more flexible ownership cycle, allowing you to swap cars regularly. Most customers switch around 3 years into a 4 year agreement, using any positive equity as a deposit for their next car. 
		- At the end of your term you can:
			- Return the car (must be in good condition and within mileage limits or you will be subject to charges).
			- Pay the GMFV to keep the car.
			- Part-exchange for a new vehicle.
	- **What are the risks**
		- In some situations a PCP agreement might become more expensive in the long run when compared to a HP. A smaller deposit at the beginning of your agreement can also cause issues should you exceed the limits of your agreement with financial penalties for excess mileage etc. Remember, unless you pay the balloon at the end of the agreement in full, the car isn’t yours. Exceeding contract limits can have financial repercussions.
- **HP with Balloon** (Lease Purchase) – HP with a balloon, sometimes known as Lease Purchase, is more a specialist agreement than a PCP, typically available on cars of higher value, but requires qualification from the lender. This more exclusive access means typically the balloon is as unique as the vehicle you are financing. I will work with the lender to secure a balloon for your vehicle, then build a finance package to suit your personal circumstances, this isn’t your off the shelf finance!
	- 
		- 
			- 
				- **At the end of your finance deal, you can:**
- Sell the car to settle the remaining loan.
- Refinance the balloon payment.
- Complete the final balloon payment in full to own the car outright.
- This structure offers flexibility and a stable final payment. However, failing to settle the balance can impact your credit rating. Note that you cannot hand the car back to the lender like in a PCP agreement.
	- **What are the benefits**
		- There are multiple benefits to this type of agreement, depending on the vehicle and your situation.
		- Flexible deposit options
		- Fixed monthly payments
		- Quality advice and guidance
		- Early settlement support
	- **What are the Risks**
		- As with all types of borrowing, there are risks to note before taking out a Lease Purchase agreement.
		- Only available on cars over a certain value
		- You must qualify for the product with the lender.
		- You will not own the vehicle until the final balloon payment is made.
- **Interest-Only Car Finance** is a type of car loan where you only pay the interest on the loan for a specified period. Typically, available to those adding in a substantial deposit so that all that is left to pay is the interest as the amount borrowed would equal the final payment.
	- **Key features:**
		- **Lower Payments:** During the agreement, your monthly payments are lower because you’re only covering the interest. The capital is deferred until the end.
		- **Equity Control:** Since you’ve already paid a substantial deposit, the equity in the vehicle is more accessible when switching cars in the future. However, it’s important to note that you are effectively paying the depreciation upfront.
	- **Risks to note:**
		- Since you’re not paying down the principal during the interest only period, you won’t build further equity in the car unless its market value increases.
- **What is Regulated Finance**
	- A regulated finance agreement is a credit agreement governed by the Consumer Credit Act 1974. It typically applies when a lender provides credit to an individual or small partnership, with PCP and Hire Purchase both falling into this category. These agreements require the lender to meet specific legal obligations, including clear disclosure of terms, the right to cancel, and protections against unfair lending practices. This kind of finance is utilised by the majority of car finance customers, offering protection throughout the agreement, with less prerequisites to limit who can apply. PCP is the UK’s most popular form of regulated finance, with over 80% of our customers opting for a PCP agreement with us.
- **What is Non-Regulated Finance?**
	- 
		- A non regulated finance agreement in the UK is a credit agreement that falls outside the scope of the Consumer Credit Act 1974. These agreements are not subject to the same consumer protections, giving lenders and qualified borrowers more flexibility. Non Regulated is available for corporate entities and High Net Worth Individuals (HNWI). To qualify as a HNWI you must have £500k in assets outside your primary residence or income in excess of over £150,000 net per annum. This gives me the option to utilise our relationship with a number of commercial funders on our panel, who can offer bespoke HP \+ Balloon (Lease Purchase) terms.
