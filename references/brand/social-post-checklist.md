Content Optimisation Check List

This checklist ensures your posts are deep, engaging, memorable, and action-driven, aligning with the feedback received.

1. Hook & Reader Retention 

☐ Does the first 1-2 lines immediately tell the reader why they should care? 

☐ Does it avoid fluff (e.g., small talk, weather updates) and get to the point? 

☐ Does it create curiosity, tension, or an emotional pull to keep them reading?\#

2. Depth & Substance 

☐ Does the post go beyond surface-level advice (i.e., not just quick tips or reminders)? 

☐ Does it explain the why behind the advice, not just the what? 

☐ Does it describe real-world outcomes or transformations to make the message tangible? ☐ Does it address different types of buyers (e.g., logical learners, story-driven learners)? 

3. Engagement & Psychological Triggers 

☐ Does the post use at least one psychological trigger (e.g., curiosity, authority, social proof, FOMO)? 

☐ Does it avoid sounding overly promotional while still leading towards an offer or service? ☐ Does it contrast pain vs. solution to create urgency and relevance? 

☐ Is it written in an engaging, natural tone rather than a corporate or robotic style?

4. Storytelling & Reader Connection

☐ Does it tell a story or give a relatable example to make the lesson more engaging? 

☐ Does it make the reader envision themselves achieving the outcome you're describing? 

☐ Does it avoid being self-focused and instead frame the message around the reader's needs?

5. Call to Action & Next Steps 

☐ Does the post clearly state the next step the reader should take? 

☐ Does it include a compelling CTA (e.g., ask a question, invite engagement, direct towards a solution)? 

☐ Does it give a strong reason for them to act now instead of later?

6. Formatting & Readability

☐ Does the post avoid large blocks of text (max 2-3 lines per paragraph)? 

☐ Is it formatted for easy scanning (e.g., bold highlights, bullet points where useful)?

☐ Does every sentence add value (i.e., no filler or redundant phrases)? --- 

7. Unique & Differentiated Messaging 

☐ Could this post be mistaken for someone else’s content, or does it sound uniquely like you? 

☐ Does it avoid common clichés and generic phrasing? 

☐ Does it introduce a fresh perspective or challenge a common belief?

Final Test: 

The One-Sentence Summary If this post were summarized in one sentence, would it still be interesting and worth reading?
