# Social Content Pillars

Five content types that cover everything worth posting. Every piece of content fits one of these.

---

## 1. Deal Spotlight

**What it is:** A real deal you've recently done or can do. Makes it tangible.

**Why it works:** Dealers and consumers see that you can fund the unusual stuff. It builds belief.

**Examples:**
- 2010 Audi R8 on HP with a balloon — most brokers won't touch it, you can
- Ferrari 296 Speciale on bespoke interest-only with enhanced balloon
- LHD American muscle car on HP
- Provisional licence holder funded on PCP
- New startup company — 3 months trading — funded on business HP
- Plant and machinery (telehandler, excavator) on commercial HP
- Campervan conversion on HP

**Image type:** Vehicle showcase (car photo + headline + "swipe for finance example")

**Copy tone:** Confident, specific, real. Not "we can fund anything" — tell them the actual deal.

**FCA note:** If you include figures, use the representative example disclaimer.

---

## 2. Rate Watch

**What it is:** Lender rate changes, new products, improved terms, promotional rates.

**Why it works:** Dealers need to know when something gets better (or worse). This is intelligence they can't get elsewhere.

**Examples:**
- "Northridge have dropped their minimum rate to X% — worth knowing for your next proposal"
- "Alphera have launched a new prestige product for vehicles over £50k"
- "Haydock are offering extended terms on commercial this month"

**Image type:** Rate/Lender Update template — clean, minimal, rate prominently displayed

**Copy tone:** Straight-to-the-point. What changed, what it means for the dealer.

**FCA note:** Always include representative APR if a rate is mentioned publicly.

---

## 3. Lender News

**What it is:** Policy updates, appetite changes, lender-specific intelligence.

**Why it works:** Dealers don't have time to track every lender. You do. This positions you as the expert.

**Examples:**
- "CA Autobank have tightened criteria on sole traders — here's what they want to see"
- "Close Brothers are now accepting vehicles up to 15 years old on HP"
- "MotoNovo have updated their debit back provisions — affects older deals"

**Image type:** Rate/Lender Update template or plain text graphic

**Copy tone:** Informative, useful, specific. Like a broker who actually knows the panel.

**FCA note:** Short disclaimer only — no rates mentioned.

---

## 4. Underwriting Masterclass

**What it is:** Educational posts showing what you can fund that others struggle with. Builds your reputation as the broker who finds a way.

**Why it works:** Most dealers have been told "no" somewhere else. Seeing you know this territory makes them pick up the phone.

**Examples:**
- "Voters roll — why it matters and how we work around it"
- "VATable vehicles — how deferred VAT works on a commercial deal"
- "What's a balloon and why would you use one on a 2012 prestige?"
- "Interest-only finance — when does it make sense?"
- "HNWI deals — how non-regulated finance works for wealthy customers"
- "Bad credit with a story — what I need to make a case"
- "Tricky income profiles — self-employed, contractors, multiple income streams"

**Image type:** Underwriting Tip template — clean dark background, strong headline, short explanation

**Copy tone:** Educational, authoritative, plain English. No jargon. Teach the dealer something they'll use.

**FCA note:** Short disclaimer. These are educational, not promotions — but always include.

---

## 5. Industry Insight

**What it is:** Commentary on broader motor finance and market trends. Shows you're thinking bigger than just the next deal.

**Why it works:** Builds credibility and presence. Positions you as someone worth following, not just transacting with.

**Examples:**
- FCA consumer duty updates and what they mean for dealers
- Used car market commentary — how it affects finance approvals
- EV finance — what lenders are doing differently
- End of plate changes and how it affects proposals
- Interest rate environment and its effect on PCP monthly payments

**Image type:** Brand awareness template or plain text card

**Copy tone:** Thoughtful, measured, no scaremongering. Your view, backed by what you're seeing day-to-day.

**FCA note:** Short disclaimer.

## Weekly Rhythm (suggested)

| Day | Pillar | Platform |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Rate Watch or Lender News | WhatsApp Group + LinkedIn |
| Wednesday | Deal Spotlight or Underwriting Masterclass | WhatsApp Group + Instagram |
| Friday | Industry Insight or Deal Spotlight | LinkedIn + WhatsApp Group |

Adjust based on what's happening that week. If a lender moves on rates mid-week, post it immediately — don't wait for the schedule.
